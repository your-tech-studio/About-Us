Area & Borders

Egypt covers about 1,001,450 km² and is mostly desert. It borders Libya, Sudan, Israel, the Mediterranean Sea, and the Red Sea, making it strategically located between continents.

Location
Location

Egypt is in North Africa, with the Sinai Peninsula in Asia. Its position between 22°–32° N gives it a hot and dry climate.

Landforms
Landforms

Egypt is dominated by deserts and the Sinai Mountains, with fertile land along the Nile River where most people live.

Characteristics

Egypt has a hot and dry climate, with summer temperatures often reaching up to 40°C, especially in desert regions. During the night, temperatures can drop significantly, making desert nights surprisingly cold. The country generally experiences two main seasons: a long, hot summer and a milder winter with more comfortable temperatures. Seasonal winds known as Khamsin sometimes blow from the desert, carrying hot air, dust, and sand across the country. Humidity levels are usually low in most inland areas, but they tend to be higher along the Mediterranean coast and around the Nile River, where the presence of water influences the local climate.
